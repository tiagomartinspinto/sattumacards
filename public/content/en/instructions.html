Here are two possible ways to play with the web game cards. The idea is that each card, together with the other cards turned onto the table, defines an imagined teaching situation.

The players' task is to imagine and describe, using the information given by the cards, what kind of teaching situation is taking place and how it might continue or change as the cards change.

The virtual game table and its cards appear in the same way to everyone who has joined the room. As the game becomes familiar, the group can also adapt the rules to its own needs.

Game A is guided by the interface. Game B is a freer variation that can be played socially with the same shared table.

For example, players may agree that a card is occasionally or in a certain situation empty or a "joker", allowing the text on the card to be changed into something else.

GAME A

Start a round by choosing one card from each of the six decks. Players take turns dragging the top card of a deck onto its matching place on the table, or the host can press the Sattuma button to deal a random six-card scenario automatically.

In the manual flow, each player places one card and then turns it over before the turn moves on. When there are six cards on the table, the group reads them aloud and describes together what kind of teaching situation they create. The discussion may be timed if the group wants to keep the game moving.

After the discussion, the host can start a replacement round. The next player replaces one card on the table with a new card from the same deck. The new card is then turned over and read aloud, and the players describe how this change affects the teaching situation.

The game continues by alternating between discussion and single-card replacement. The group may agree in advance how many replacement rounds to play, or continue until the story no longer changes in a meaningful way.

The Start over button clears the shared table and returns the room to the beginning of a new round. Only the host can reset the table, deal a random scenario, change the timer, or start a replacement round.

GAME B

The game can also be played so that cards are dealt from the six decks onto the game table as before, but all players interpret the turned cards together more freely.

Once the row has been spoken aloud as a story of a teaching event, one player replaces or turns a new card and the players again imagine and describe aloud the change caused by it.

Telling the story together always produces several possibilities, and in each round the players must agree what kind of teaching event and shared story is formed by the current row of turned cards.
